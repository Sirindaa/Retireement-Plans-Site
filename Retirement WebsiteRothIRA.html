Roth Retirement Plan

What is a Roth IRA?

A Roth IRA (individual retirement account) is a retirement plan under US law that is generally not taxed, if you meet its requirements.

Who is Eligible?

Single or head of household: You must earn less than $118,000 to fully contribute to a Roth IRA

Married filing jointly or a qualified widow(er): Must earn less than $186,000 to fully contribute to a Roth IRA

Married filing separately: You must earn less than $10,000 to fully contribute to a Roth IRA. (Note: Those married filing separately can use the limits for single people if they have not lived with their spouse in the past year).

How to apply for a Roth IRA

1. Make sure you are eligible.

2. Decide where to open the account.

3. Send in paperwork or open your account online.

4. Make your investment decisions.

5. Set up your monthly or annual contribution schedule.

6.Check on the account at least once a year.

Pros to opening a Roth IRA Account:

You get tax-free income in retirement

Roth IRAs offer flexibility

You can contribute after age 70 1/2

Your heirs benefit

Cons to opening a Roth Ira Account:

High-income individuals cannot contribute to a Roth( need to earn less than $118,000)

No immediate tax deduction
