401K Retirement Plan

What is a 401k?

401(k) plan is the tax-qualified, defined-contribution pension account defined in subsection 401(k) of the Internal Revenue Code. it’s a retirement plan offered by employers, and is also available if you are self-employed.

Who is eligible?

Usually full time employees. The most restrictive case an employer can make 401k eligibility is to require an employee to be at least 21 years and have one year of service with at least 1000 hours worked

How to open an account:

Your employer opens the account. If the company’s 401k is expensive and no matching, opt out and set up an independent retirement account or Roth IRA on your own.

Pros to applying for a 401K

The sooner you start saving - the more time your money has to grow. That’s compounding.

You will gain a sense of achievement and maybe even momentum to take the next step, whether it’s getting back on track after an event in your life has slowed your savings, or creating a plan for living out your dreams in retirement.

Federal law protection: never be afraid that an employer will steal your money

Matching funds: If you earn $50,000 per year and contribute 3% or $1,500; your employer could also contribute $1,500 on your behalf. You’ll have $3,000 in total contribution and have received 100% return on your $1,500 investment.

Cons to applying for a 401K

Account Fees

Early withdrawal fees: 10% penalty for taking money before age of 59.5 year
