HSA Retirement Plan

What is an HSA Retirement plan?

An HSA, also known as a Health Savings Account, allows you to set aside money for qualified health care expenses on a pre-tax basis. This means you can contribute money to your account without having to pay taxes on them.

Who is eligible?

People Enrolled in a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) and no other health coverage, such as Medicare, military health benefits, medical FSAs, or another health plan.

How to open an account

You can open an account through your bank, your healthcare provider, or your employer. Contact them to find out the next steps to open your account

Pros to applying for a HSA

You own the account and all its contributions, even if you change employers, change health plans, or retire.

Money you contribute is not taxed and you can earn interest tax-free on your account.

The entire HSA balance rolls over year after year

Cons to applying for a HSA

The possibility of losing investments to market changes

HSA transfer fees: note this depends on who you open the account with

Recordkeeping: you have to keep track of all your receipts to prove that the money was used for qualified health expenses

What are some examples of qualified health expenses?

-Acupuncture

-Therapy and counseling

-Dental treatments

-Contact lens

-Surgery

What are the limits on contributions?

For individuals, it is $3,400, and for families it is $6,750
